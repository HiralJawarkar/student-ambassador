# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

## General Online Safety
- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across platforms.
- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps, and UPI apps. This adds a second layer even if your password is stolen.
- [ ] **Never click links in unsolicited messages** — whether SMS, WhatsApp, or email. Always go directly to the official website or app instead of tapping a link.
- [ ] **Verify before you trust** — if someone calls claiming to be from a bank, company, or government body, hang up and call back on the official helpline number found on their website.
- [ ] **Keep your apps and phone OS updated** — security patches in updates protect you from known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.
- [ ] **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks** — never log into banking, UPI, or email on café or college hostel open Wi-Fi. Use mobile data or a trusted VPN.

## Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)
- [ ] **Scanning a QR code always means YOU are paying — never receiving.** There is no such thing as "scan to receive a refund" in UPI. If anyone asks you to scan a code to get money, it is a scam.
- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay, PhonePe, or customer support. Legitimate services will never ask for these.
- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app to minimise losses in case of compromise. Most apps allow you to set this under settings or security.
- [ ] **Double-check the recipient's name before confirming any UPI payment** — fraudsters use names and profile pictures that closely mimic real contacts or businesses.

## Specific to College Students
- [ ] **Be cautious of "scholarship," "part-time job," or "internship" offers on WhatsApp groups** that ask for a registration fee or bank details upfront — these are common scams targeting students.
- [ ] **Do not post your phone number, address, or Aadhaar details publicly** on social media, college forums, or group chats, even casually.

## If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately
**National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online 24/7 |
**Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Call immediately after financial fraud for the best chance of freezing funds |
**Your Bank's Helpline** | Report unauthorised transactions within 24 hours to limit liability |

**Act within the first hour** of a UPI fraud — calling **1930** quickly can help authorities freeze the fraudulent account before money is transferred further.
