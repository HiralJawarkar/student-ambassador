# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud
**Type:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud
**Scenario:** Realistic Fictional Scenario (based on widely reported fraud patterns in India)

## "The Fake Refund Call" — A College Student's Story
Riya, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, ordered a study lamp through an e-commerce app. Two days later, she received a call from someone claiming to be a "customer care executive" saying her order had been cancelled and she was eligible for a ₹499 refund.

### How It Happened — Step by Step
1. **The Hook:** The caller knew Riya's name, order ID, and phone number — details likely scraped from a data breach or fake seller listing.
2. **Building Trust:** He spoke professionally, used the company's name, and sent a fake customer care link via WhatsApp.
3. **The Trap:** Riya was asked to "accept" the refund by scanning a QR code sent to her phone.
4. **The Exploit:** She scanned the code — which was actually a **payment request**, not a credit. ₹4,900 was instantly debited from her account.
5. **The Vanish:** The caller disconnected. The number was unreachable within minutes.

### Who Is Usually Targeted
First-time online shoppers, college students, elderly users, and anyone unfamiliar with how UPI transactions work.

### Consequences
- Immediate financial loss (often ₹2,000–₹50,000)
- Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty recovering funds due to instant transaction finality
- In some cases, full bank account compromise if UPI PIN is shared

> **Key Insight:** In UPI, you **never scan a QR code to receive money.** Scanning always means paying.
